Discover Norway

Land of fjords, northern lights, and midnight sun

Why Norway is Special to Me

Norway touched my soul in ways I never expected. My first encounter with the Norwegian wilderness was during a summer hike in the Lofoten Islands, where jagged peaks plunged into emerald waters under the golden light of the midnight sun. That moment of awe was just the beginning of my love affair with this spectacular country.

What makes Norway truly special is its profound connection to nature. The concept of "friluftsliv" (open-air living) permeates Norwegian culture, and I embraced it wholeheartedly - from kayaking through silent fjords at dawn to cross-country skiing under the northern lights. The raw, untamed beauty of the landscape is matched only by the warmth of the people, who welcomed me with their quiet hospitality.

Some of my most cherished memories include sailing past towering cliffs in Geirangerfjord, learning about Viking history in Oslo, and sharing stories with locals over a pot of steaming fårikål (lamb stew). Norway taught me to appreciate the simple joys of nature and the value of sustainable living - lessons I carry with me to this day.

How to Get to Norway

Norway is well-connected with various transportation options:

By Air

Major international airports include Oslo Gardermoen (OSL), Bergen (BGO), and Tromsø (TOS). Direct flights available from many European cities and some intercontinental destinations.

By Sea

Ferries connect Norway to Denmark, Germany, and the UK. The famous Hurtigruten coastal ferry offers both transportation and spectacular scenic cruising.

By Land

Train connections from Sweden (Stockholm/Gothenburg to Oslo) and bus routes from neighboring countries. Norway's scenic rail network is spectacular once you're in the country.

Seasonal Activities

Norway offers incredible experiences year-round:

Winter Wonders

Northern lights viewing, dog sledding, skiing in world-class resorts, and experiencing the magic of Christmas markets.

Summer Adventures

Midnight sun hikes, fjord kayaking, glacier walking, and coastal road trips along spectacular routes.

Other Amazing Places to Visit in Norway

Beyond Oslo and Bergen, Norway's landscapes will take your breath away:

Tromsø

The "Gateway to the Arctic" offers northern lights, midnight sun, and a vibrant cultural scene in a stunning northern setting.

Ålesund

Art Nouveau architecture meets spectacular fjords and nearby islands in this beautiful coastal town.

Stavanger

Base for exploring Pulpit Rock and Kjerag, with a charming old town and excellent museums.

Trondheim

Historic city with Norway's magnificent Nidaros Cathedral and vibrant student culture.
