Childhood Years

Born in a little neighborhood where everything started.1 My life as a kid with no knowledge of everything started my whole fate.2 Cry baby as a kid, just an innocent child who does not yet know the dream of life.3 Wandering outside the house to gather future friends on the road.4 Make enemies but then become friends in the end.5 It is more solid to be friends with those who have become your enemies because you can say that you are true to each other.6 As I grew up, I gradually learned about right and wrong.7 It's good to be young, especially if you're just starting to learn about things in the world.8 Sometimes I can't avoid being stubborn because all I used to know was to play, sleep, and eat.9 I still remember my friends when I was a child.10 I am very grateful that they were a part of my youth.11 Although my friends and I often fight, our solid friendship is still more dominant because in the end, we are the only ones together whenever we are outside our house.12 As I grow up, I also know little by little how this is and how that is.13 As a stubborn child, I gradually understood that what my parents were telling me was right.14 It's wrong for someone like me to make mistakes; I'm just a human being like most people.15 I miss being a kid so much because I enjoy every moment more as I grow up.16 If I had to choose if I wanted to be a child again, I would agree because I can say that it is better to be a child without thinking about problems.17 Childhood taught me the value of simple joys and unconditional friendships.18 Those early years shaped my curiosity about the world around me.19 Looking back, I realize how those childhood experiences formed the foundation of who I am today.20

Teenage Years

Fast forward to my teenage years, when I turned 13 years old.1 I was so happy because I was waiting for this moment.2 I always wanted to be a teenager because I was very curious when I was a child about what life is like as a teenager.3 Little by little, I realized as a teenager that my friends are decreasing, and they are being replaced by those who become my friends at school.4 Because there really is no permanent thing or person in your life here in the world we all stand in.5 You also learn more, especially in life, because you interact with different kinds of people in school.6 It's also fun to be a teenager because you know little by little what life will be like when you finish school.7 You will have enemies, and you can't tell if they will be your friends like when we were kids.8 We don't all think the same way, so you can't just go with anyone at our age.9 For me, teenagers are the most dangerous and the happiest part of our lives because we are often tempted by day.12 Many temptations came to me, and I thank myself because I was able to control all the temptations around me as a teenager.13 I am very thankful for the people around me when I was a teenager adolescence helped me develop resilience and problem-solving skills.18 Looking back, I appreciate how my teenage years balanced freedom with growing responsibilities.19 The friendships formed during College Years

As a college student, it can be said that this is where my journey in life began.1 Because of this, we will know what we will be when the time comes when I graduate from school.2 Others are right in saying that college life is very different compared to our teenage life.3 I also realized that we are the only ones who will make the direction of our lives.4 This college life is also like a survival game because you are the only one who will save yourself in times when you are drowning.5 You have no one to rely on but yourself whenever there are trials that go through our college life.6 It depends on our own hands what we will be in the future; our parents only help us in our studies, like expenses, bills, etc.7 But in the end, we are still the ones who can do or say what we are or what we will achieve in the future.8 I also met a lot of college friends and different people in what I am now.9 As a 2nd-year college student, I can say that it is difficult to get along with other people or schoolmates because people think so differently now that I am in college compared to when I was in high school.10 Every day I think about my life when I was just in high school, which was so much fun and almost no problem to think about.11 I am very grateful to my friends since I was in high school because I learned a lot from them and experienced things that made me happy that I thought I would never be able to do in my whole life.12 College has taught me the importance of time management and self-discipline.13 I've learned to balance academic responsibilities with personal growth and social life.14 The challenges of higher education have pushed me to develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills.15 I've discovered new interests and potential career paths through my coursework.16 College has shown me the value of networking and building professional relationships.17 I've learned to appreciate different perspectives through interactions with diverse classmates.18 The independence of college life has helped me become more self-reliant and confident.19 These years are shaping me into the person I will become in my professional and personal life.20
